About Me

I was most recently a Software Engineer for Algorand, where I worked on programmability, smart-contract languages, client libraries, and developer infrastructure for Web3 apps. I am broadly interested in software engineering, distributed systems, and blockchain. I am also interested in Computer Science education, and frequently teach as an Adjunct in the Boston area.

Prior to this, I was a graduate student at the University of Toronto's Department of Computer Science, where I worked with Prof. Fan Long. I was also briefly at Google and Amazon where I worked in the Global Networking group, AWS DynamoDB, and IoT Core.

Outside of work, I like playing my trombone, kicking around soccer balls, and skimming self-help books.

You can reach me by email or on LinkedIn if you'd like to talk!

Recordr.ai: Face-tracking Video Recorder

In a team of 4, I developed a face-tracking video recording app for my capstone project. We used FaceNet and PoseNet, hosted on an EC2 instance, to recognize the speakers' faces and detect gesture-based commands. To communicate with the neural nets, we used AWS IoT to receive real-time commands from the EC2 and relay them to an Arduino motor with a smartphone mount.
