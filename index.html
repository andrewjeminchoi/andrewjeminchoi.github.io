About Me

I currently work as an Engineer for Algorand, where I work on programmability and smart-contract languages for blockchain platforms. I am broadly interested in software engineering, distributed systems, and blockchain.

Prior to this, I was a graduate student at the University of Toronto's Department of Computer Science, where I worked with Prof. Fan Long. I was also a Software Intern at Google and Amazon where I worked on Software Defined Network (SDN) routing, AWS DynamoDB, and IoT authentication systems.

You can reach me by email or on LinkedIn if you'd like to talk!

Projects

Friendle

In a team of 4, I worked on the backend for a matching/hangout suggestion app to help those with social anxiety. We deployed the backend on GCP Cloud Functions (serverless) and integrated it with Firestore. Our project received Telus' Best Mental Health Hack and 2nd place overall at UoftHacks VIII.

Check us out on Devpost!

Recordr.ai: Face-tracking Video Recorder

In a team of 4, I developed a face-tracking video recording app for my capstone project. We used FaceNet and PoseNet, hosted on an EC2 instance, to recognize the speakers' faces and detect gesture-based commands. To communicate with the neural nets, we used AWS IoT to receive real-time commands from the EC2 and relay them to an Arduino motor with a smartphone mount.
You can see a demo of the project here: Google Drive

Crunch: A reaction-based video game (Verilog)

My friend and I created a game where users control an alligator to avoid obstacles. The game was programmed on Altera DE1 SoCs to have graphics and varying challenges for players.
